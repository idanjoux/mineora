01

Opportunity Overview

Project Corona is a gold-silver-copper epithermal vein system located in Badiraguato Municipality, Sinaloa State, within the prolific Sierra Madre Occidental precious metals belt — the same geological corridor hosting McEwen Mining's El Gallo complex (566K oz Au M&I) and Mincore's Tameapa porphyry Cu-Mo deposit.

An 18-hole, 2,618m NI 43-101 diamond drill program (Oct 2011, Malahoff & Brittliffe, P.Geo.) was completed across three named vein systems — Bohemia, Indio, and Corona — with 7 of 7 pads intersecting target structures. Best intercept: 5m @ 39 g/t Au · 93.3 g/t Ag · 2.13% Cu (Hole 11LD011, Bohemia structure). Strike extent has been tested over 380m; the northern slope and depth remain entirely untested.

No formal mineral resource estimate exists — all resource upside is uncommitted. The asset is Phase II-ready, requiring seed capital to complete a drill campaign across the Bohemia and Indio systems and establish a maiden NI 43-101 resource. The property is held by Astral Mining Corporation.

With gold at $4,784/oz and copper at multi-year highs, the mineral potential of an untested 930m strike on a proven epithermal system has rarely been more compelling for Phase II capital.

03

Key Competitive Advantages

NI 43-101 Certified
A fully compliant NI 43-101 technical report was prepared by two independent qualified persons (Malahoff & Brittliffe, P.Geo.) in November 2012. This is an exceptional de-risking credential for an early-stage asset in Mexico, eliminating a costly first step for incoming capital.
100% Drill Success Rate
Seven of seven drill pads intersected target vein structures in the Phase I program — confirming grade, continuity, and spatial control of the vein system. Structural predictability is a key requirement for successful resource estimation and Phase II targeting.
Producing District Neighbours
McEwen Mining's El Gallo (40 km SW, producing), Mincore's Tameapa Cu-Mo porphyry (20 km SE, PEA complete), and GoldGroup/DynaResource's San Jose de Gracia (60 km NW, NI 43-101 resource) confirm the district's geological productivity and investability.
930m Untested Strike
The Indio vein system has been traced 930m on surface; Phase I tested only 380m of the strike length. The northern slope, and all depth extensions below current drill holes, are entirely untested — representing genuine uncommitted resource upside.
Gold + Copper Co-Product
The Indio structure contains a massive chalcopyrite lens (1,230 g/t Ag, 8.91% Cu in one intercept) alongside gold mineralization, providing a polymetallic economics profile at a time when copper demand from electrification is at record levels.
Historic Underground Workings
The Bohemia structure has 70m of historic underground development confirming early-period high-grade exploitation. Combined with Phase I drill results and surface grab samples of 60.3 g/t Au, this indicates exceptional near-surface grade concentration.
